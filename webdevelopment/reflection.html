As a Computer Science major at the University of Missouri-Columbia, my academic journey has been created by a broad list of courses that provide me with a foundation in technology, engineering, and communication. Through my classes, I have gained knowledge in algorithms, game development, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity. My participation in the Rocket League Esports Club has taught me how valuable teamwork, leadership, and strategic thinking is.

As I am approaching my senior year, I am focused on building real-world experience through internships and industry opportunities. I am actively seeking internships in software engineering, information technology, cybersecurity, and game development, aiming to apply the skills I've perfected throughout my academic journey. These experiences will not only help improve my expertise but also will allow me to contribute to more impactful projects within these fields. As Competition Manager and Captain of the Gold Team, I am committed to fostering teamwork, leadership, and strategic improvement. The club will serve as both a platform for improving my leadership skills and a source of inspiration for improvement in both my academic work and future career. My long-term goal is to not only excel in the aspects of my career but also to give back to the community, potentially teaching the next generation of leaders within the club. Looking forward to my career goals, I am applying what I have learned to tackle complex challenges in software engineering and cybersecurity. I am also excited to continue exploring the combination of game development and artificial intelligence, areas where I hope that I can contribute to innovations on both ends. Ultimately, my career goals revolve around blending my passion for technology, gaming, and cybersecurity to create a meaningful impact in our society. Whether as a software engineer, cybersecurity expert, or game developer, I strive to continue growing both personally and professionally, with the ultimate goal of contributing to advancements in the field and potentially mentoring the next generation of tech innovators.
